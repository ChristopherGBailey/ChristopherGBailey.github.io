Work selected as 'Hot Paper' in [Angewandte Chemie](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/ange.202301678). Polydopamine free radicals are observed to be photo-responsive under visible light irradiation, amplifying semiquinone radical population and modifying redox potential. Using polydopamine nanoparticles, the researchers photo-initiate free-radical polymerization of vinylic monomers via sensitization of a diphenyliodonium photo-initiator. This work offers important insights into melanin-like materials and presents PDA as a promising photosensitizer.
